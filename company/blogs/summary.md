## Recent Featured Articles

### 1. "7 Signs You Should Update Your Ad Creative"
**Published:** June 19, 2025
**Author:** Evan Dunn, Head of Growth @ Pixis
**URL:** https://pixis.ai/blog/7-signs-you-should-update-your-ad-creative

**Topic:**
Warning signals indicating when to refresh ad creative to combat audience fatigue and maintain campaign performance.

**Key Takeaways:**
- **CTR Decline:** Compare CTRs over 7-day windows to baseline; persistent dips below average signal fatigue
- **Rising Costs:** Watch for CPC/CPA increases of 15-20% above 30-day median for 7+ days
- **High Frequency, Low Conversion:** Extra impressions without improved results indicate creative burnout
- **Creative-Landing Disconnect:** Clicks without conversions suggest misalignment between ad promise and page delivery
- **Contextual Relevance:** Out-of-sync seasonal or cultural messaging hurts performance
- **Impression Drops:** Falling delivery despite unchanged budgets means platform is downgrading relevance
- **Negative Sentiment:** Comments like "Why do I see this ad everywhere?" are early warning signs

**Recommended System:**
- Monthly performance audits
- Frequent A/B testing
- Smart audience segmentation
- Seasonal content calendar
- Weekly creative rotation to prevent fatigue

### 2. "How to Scale Multichannel Marketing Without Wasting Budget"
**Published:** December 19, 2025
**Author:** Kristen Pecka, Head of Stellar Marketing @ Pixis
**URL:** https://pixis.ai/blog/how-to-scale-multichannel-marketing-strategies-without-wasting-budget

**Topic:**
Scaling multichannel campaigns efficiently while avoiding the 30-40% budget waste from duplicate reach, misattribution, and poor creative fit.

**Key Problems Identified:**
1. **Channel Overlap:** Paying to reach same person on Facebook, Instagram, and email simultaneously
2. **Attribution Gaps:** Funding channels that look busy but don't convert
3. **Creative Fatigue:** Copy-paste messaging across platforms reduces engagement everywhere

**Strategic Approach:**
1. **Sequential Testing:** Master one channel → validate on second → expand systematically
2. **Multi-Touch Attribution:** Connect touchpoints into customer journeys to see which combinations drive conversions
3. **Creative Adaptation:** Keep core message consistent but change format/tone per platform
4. **AI-Led Budget Allocation:** Automate daily shifts toward performing channels instead of monthly reviews
5. **Frequency Caps:** Limit how often same person sees ads across channels

**Critical Metrics:**
- **Blended ROAS:** Return across all channels combined
- **Incremental CPA:** Additional cost when adding new channel
- **Channel Saturation Index:** Point where more budget stops improving results
- **Time-to-Touchpoint:** How long customers take to move between channels before converting

**Tools Mentioned:**
- AI budget optimizers for real-time spend redistribution
- Creative versioning engines for automatic adaptation
- Unified attribution dashboards for customer journey visibility

### 3. "From Tariffs to Whatever's Next: Why Marketing Leaders Are Betting on AI"
**Published:** April 17, 2025
**Author:** Jason Widup, SVP of Marketing @ Pixis
**URL:** https://pixis.ai/blog/from-tariffs-to-whatevers-next-betting-on-ai

**Topic:**
How AI-powered marketing systems enable adaptability and resilience in response to market disruptions like tariffs, supply chain issues, and economic volatility.

**Context:**
- Conference Board's Consumer Confidence Index fell 7.2 points in March 2025
- Consumer expectations hit 12-year low
- 50%+ of U.S. advertisers expect budget cuts of 6-10%
- Some facing cuts up to 20%

**Key Distinction: AI vs. Automation**

| **Automation** | **True AI** |
|---|---|
| Executes pre-determined actions | Adapts based on real-time data |
| Requires manual rule updates | Learns and evolves autonomously |
| Works when rules are clear | Thrives in volatile conditions |
| Static optimization | Continuous improvement |

**AI Capabilities in Practice:**

1. **Audience Discovery:** AI finds unexpected patterns (e.g., "people who watch unboxing videos after 10pm") that human planners wouldn't consider

2. **Real-Time Budget Optimization:** Automatically shifts spend when geo-targeted campaigns behave differently due to regional tariff impacts

3. **Creative Fatigue Detection:** Monitors scroll speed, CTR, drop-offs to rotate creative before performance tanks

4. **Adaptive Forecasting:** Uses multiple budget-pacing models in parallel, stress-tests in AI simulator, promotes best-performing approaches

**Counter-Intuitive Strategies:**

Experienced marketers "zig when others zag":
- **Invest in awareness** when attribution breaks down (incrementality beats last-click ROAS)
- **Focus on loyalty** when price increases shrink addressable market
- **Design customer experiences** that maintain pricing power despite cost pressures

**Quote from Rupesh Sharma (Realtime Agency):**
> "Upper funnel work — brand, loyalty, values — puts you on that list. That's what you're investing in."

**The Resilience Advantage:**

AI-powered teams don't just react faster—they build adaptability into workflows:
- Key optimization, targeting, and creative workflows are AI-enhanced or fully delegated
- Result: Creative problem-solving instead of constant firefighting
- Teams focus on strategy while AI handles repetitive optimization
